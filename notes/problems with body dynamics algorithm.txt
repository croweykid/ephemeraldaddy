Your most valid & useful points:

4. It ignores asymmetry.
Your dictionary treats a pair as one unit. Real charts often do not.
Example:
Saturn square Moon may burden the Moon far more than it “burdens Saturn”
Jupiter trine Sun may strengthen the Sun’s functioning more than it changes Jupiter’s role
Mercury conjunct Neptune may blur Mercury more than it empowers Neptune
So there are really two questions:
what is the pair dynamic
how does the aspect affect body A specifically and body B specifically
Your current model answers only the first.

8. It assumes “benefic” and “malefic” pair logic is universally stable.
That is partly true, but not enough to be safe.

Example:
Mars can be highly enabling in charts that require assertion, severance, speed, immunity to dithering
Saturn can be enabling in charts that are otherwise diffuse, manic, inflated, or porous
Jupiter can be destabilizing when it inflates nonsense rather than capacity
So pair priors should probably be continuous values, not categorical labels.

10. It ignores aspect pattern context.
A single aspect rarely acts alone.
Examples:
a square inside a grand trine-heavy chart behaves differently from the same square in a chart built on oppositions
a conjunction tied into a T-square is not just “this pair fused”
a trine feeding a larger avoidance pattern may be pleasant but not productive
Your model is pairwise. Charts are networks.
